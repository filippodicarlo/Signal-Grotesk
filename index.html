Signal Grotesk is a variable grotesque typeface designed for clarity at any scale. Built on a geometric skeleton with humanist corrections, it balances neutrality and personality — suitable for editorial, branding, and interface contexts.

The typeface was developed as part of an academic research project exploring the possibilities of variable font technology in contemporary type design. It is released as open source and free to use.

SAMPLE TEXT

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. Sphinx of black quartz, judge my vow. Pack my box with five dozen liquor jugs. How vexingly quick daft zebras jump. The five boxing wizards jump quickly. Jackdaws love my big sphinx of quartz. Signal Grotesk was built for precision — from the tension of its thin strokes to the confidence of its bold weights. It speaks clearly at 8pt and loudly at 800px. Typography is not decoration. It is communication.

The typeface's main feature is its variable structure on two axes: the first adjusts the weight from Light 300 to Extra Bold 700, while the second axis modifies the x-height of lowercase letters, allowing fine control over readability at different sizes and contexts.

Signal Grotesk is currently at version 1.0 and under active development. If you have feedback or find issues, feel free to get in touch.
